Pani Puri

A simple pani puri recipe for the rainy days!

Pani puri (other names: golgapa, puchka) is an Indian street food. It consists of hollow crispt puri shells filled with a mixture of spiced potatos or chikpeas. Then it is filled with a flavored water and eaten at a go.

Ingredients (pani puri stuffing)
  • 2 to 3 potatos (dahi)
  • 1 tbsp chopped coriander leaves
  • 1 tsp roasted cumin powder (jeera)
  • 1 tsp chaat masala powder
  • black salt (other salts work too!) (kaala namak)>
    • Ingredients (pani)
  • 1/2 cup chopped mint leaves (dhaniya)
  • 1 in. ginger (adrak)
    • <1i>2 chilies (work as per spice level) (mirchi) <1i>1 tsp roasted cumin powder <1i>1 tsp chaat masala powder <1i>1 1/3 cup water (personal preference rules)
  • black salt (kaala namak)
  • Also need: store-bought puris
Steps
  1. FILLING: Boil potatos, peel and chop. Mix all filling ingredients in bowl
  2. PANI: Blend all pani ingredients and then add to bowl with water. Mix and check seasoning.
  3. PANI PURI: Poke small holes into the puris. Fill with filling and dip into paani.
